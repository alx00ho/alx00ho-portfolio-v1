I currently work at Tea Hill as a Team Leader, help serve various kinds of Taiwanese teas for customers!

Growing up, I have always been captivated with creating projects with my hands. Puzzles, K'nex building sets, and the like caught my attention. I would spend hours working patiently and with a detailed eye place the pieces together. Puzzles are still one of my favorites pastimes, where I'm able to listen to enticing albums and podcasts while at work.

I discovered the Software Developer career path in high school where it fascinated the creative part of my brain and thus pursued and achieved a Bachelor of Science C.S. degree from the University of North Carolina in 2022. During my undergraduate career, I learned that time is very precious and to use it wisely between my peers, family, friends, and myself. When building my development skills, I had to learn them quickly to be active in my contributions on group projects and finishing class assignments on time.

One of my favorite projects is a Rocket League Statistics web application which complied data from professional and collegiate Rocket League matches using the ballchasing API and complied them into a readable table for users to search their favorite players and teams and compare various statistics. We were able to authenticate users and allow them to favorite players and teams and pool them into a list. Player and teams also had pop-up info cards that held their nationality and team history.

Another project that I thoroughly enjoyed was Ancient Path Adventures (APA) where my team and I created a web application for their game, Promised Land Journey. We developed a platform where APA were able to create their own modules directly related to the game and stored them in a database. These modules consisted of quizzes, videos, and readings. Teachers were allowed to use these example modules or make their own after paying a subscription to use APA's services. Students could join classes thorugh a unique access code that was randomly generated upon class created.

Outside of my technical skills, I enjoy dabbling in other creative aspects. Being a former percussionist, I try to maintain and grow my playing skills by performing with the nearby church or some rudimental drumming. I've also been trying to develop my ear and do some composition on the side. Cooking helps me connect with my Vietnamese heritage and is something that I want to share with my friends and future family. The newest of the them is 3D rendering, where I've followed the Blender Guru's tutorial on a donut animation and I plan on bringing places I've been into the digital world.
